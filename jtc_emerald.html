The Emerald Ash Borer

The emerald ash borer is a green jewel beetle, mainly found in north-eastern Asia. It feeds on ash species. Females will usually lay eggs in bark crevices on ash trees, and the larvae feed underneath the bark of ash trees. They mature to adulthood in one to two years. Strangely enough, in its native areas, it's typically found in low densities, and does not cause significant damage to trees native to the area. However, outside its native area, it is an invasive species that is extremely destructive to ash trees, especially those native to northwest Europe and North America.

Be on the lookout for this tree killing insect. If you find it, contact Jedds Tree Care immediately, we are professionals and will remove them without any further harm to your landscape or property.
